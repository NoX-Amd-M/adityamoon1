Defence: a career built around discipline, service, and an officer's commission.

From Sainik Schools and Military Schools after Class 10, to NDA and CDS after 12th — the defence track combines regular academics with physical training and a clear path to becoming a commissioned officer.

The two stages of the defence track

You don't need to go through Sainik School to join the armed forces — but it's a structured option if you want to start preparing early.

After Class 10 (optional)

Sainik School / Military School

Regular CBSE academics plus discipline, drill, and physical training — designed to prepare students for NDA later, not compulsory for a defence career.

After Class 12

NDA (National Defence Academy)

The most common entry route — PCM stream preferred for technical wings (Air Force, Navy). 3 years of joint training, then service-specific training.

After graduation

CDS (Combined Defence Services)

For graduates wanting to join as officers via Indian Military Academy, Officers Training Academy, or Naval/Air Force Academies.

Is the Defence track right for you?

Choose Defence prep if

  • You're drawn to discipline, structure, and a clear sense of purpose/service
  • You're physically fit or willing to train consistently for fitness tests
  • You can handle a regimented lifestyle, including time away from family
  • You want a respected, pensioned career with strong post-retirement options too

Reconsider Defence prep if

  • You're only considering it for family pressure or "prestige," without genuine interest
  • You have health conditions that may not meet medical fitness standards (check well in advance)
  • You strongly prefer flexibility and dislike rigid hierarchy or routine

What the selection process actually involves

Stage 1

Written exam

NDA/CDS written paper covering Maths and General Ability — conducted by UPSC twice a year.

Stage 2

SSB Interview

A 5-day Services Selection Board process testing leadership, group tasks, psychological aptitude, and a personal interview — widely considered tougher than the written exam.

Stage 3

Medical examination

Detailed fitness and medical standards specific to each service and role (especially strict for flying branches).

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to attend a Sainik School to join the NDA?

No — any 12th-pass student (with PCM for Air Force/Navy wings) can sit the NDA exam directly. Sainik/Military Schools just provide early preparation and exposure, not a mandatory step.

What's the difference between NDA and CDS?

NDA is for students straight after 12th boards; CDS is for graduates wanting to join as officers afterward. Both lead to a commissioned officer role, just at different entry ages and stages.

Is the SSB interview really as tough as people say?

It's a genuinely rigorous 5-day assessment of leadership and personality traits, not just knowledge — many strong written-exam candidates don't clear it. Preparation usually involves group discussion practice and physical fitness as much as academics.

Can women join through NDA?

Yes, NDA has been open to women candidates since 2022 across Army, Navy, and Air Force wings, following the same selection process as male candidates.
